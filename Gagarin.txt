On 12 April 1961, 27-year-old astronaut Yuri Gagarin became the first man 
to travel into space, launching to orbit aboard the Vostok 1 spacecraft. 
Gagarin circled the Earth for 1 hour and 48 minutes.
“The Earth is blue. How wonderful. It is amazing.” – Gagarin, to ground control.
12 April was declared as the International Day of Human Space Flight.
